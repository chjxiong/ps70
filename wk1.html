Final Project Ideas
Dynamic Architecture Facade
The project will focus on the development of a responsive building facade prototype that seamlessly adapts to the dynamic interplay of environmental factors such as sunlight, wind patterns, as well as pedestrian activity. There are some possible factors that might contribute to the responsiveness of the facade:
  • Sun: provides shade when needed and optimizing solar exposure to discourage reliance on eletricity-based mechanical system.

  • Sun: installs solar panels and rotates its pieces based on the sun angle to maximize energy generation.

  • Wind: engages with wind patterns to enhance natural ventilation within the building.

  • Human Interaction: responds to the presence and movement of street pedestrians, fostering a dynamic and engaging relationship between the structure and its urban context.
This prototype trys to enhance the application of building sustainability and energy efficiency in urban areas, exploring the potentials of redefining the role of building facades, not only as passive enclosures but as active contributors to the climate and human interaction.

Viability
Building a responsive facade model prototype, capable of adapting to environmental conditions or user interactions, might need a series of digital fabrication techniques, such as 3D printing, CNC machining, and laser cutting, as well as multiple electronic techniques to detect the surrounding environments. The iterative nature of digital fabrication allows for efficient testing and refinement of design parameters, in order to foster a more sustainable built environments.
