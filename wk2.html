A Box
Even though there are millions of ways to build a box that is able to hold things, a regular cuboid is be always the most acceptable way of design. So I started with the normal box. At first I directly built the 2D plan of the box, but it turned out that the printing product was not good because those teeth could not match with each other. Since those teeth were hard to imagine in a 2D plan, I started "cheating" by building a 3D model first. Then the 2D version was much easier. When I was drawing the 2D version, I further improved the box a little bit by adding a cute lid on it.

A "Box" for Books
I also made a bookshelf for myself. I started with 3D modeling in Rhino. First, I used the command sweep2 to create the surface. Then, the surface were sliced by several sections. After the surface and sections were boolean splitted, I got a series of bookshelf partition.
